# **CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION TO SIWES** {#chapter-one:-introduction-to-siwes}

1. ## INTRODUCTION TO SIWES {#introduction-to-siwes}

The Students Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES) was established in 1973 by the Industrial Training Fund (ITF) to address the gap between theoretical education and practical skills needed by graduates. Prior to SIWES, there were concerns that university and college graduates lacked the practical knowledge and experience required by employers. SIWES provides students with the opportunity to gain hands-on experience with equipment and machinery not available in their institutions, better preparing them for the workplace after graduation.

2. ## VISION STATEMENT OF SIWES {#vision-statement-of-siwes}

To Be The Leading Skills Training Organisation In Nigeria And One Of The Best In The World.

3. ## MISSION STATEMENT OF SIWES {#mission-statement-of-siwes}

To Set, Regulate Training Standards And Provide Need-Based Human Capital Development Interventions Using A Corps Of Highly Competent Professionals In Line With Global Best Practices.

4. ## CORE VALUES OF SIWES {#core-values-of-siwes}

* Commitment

* Loyalty

* Integrity

* Professionalism & Creativity

* Efficiency & Effectiveness

* Team Work

5. ## OBJECTIVES OF SIWES {#objectives-of-siwes}

The Scheme is designed to bridge the gap between employers’ expectations and actual performance of Nigerian graduates. SIWES aims to:

* Provide an avenue for students in institutions of higher learning to acquire industrial skills and experience in their course of study.

  * Expose students to work methods and techniques in handling equipment and machines that may not be available in their institutions.

  * Prepare students for Industrial Work situation they are to meet after graduation.

  * Make transition from school to the world of work easier and enhance students’ contacts for later job placement.

  * Provide Students with an opportunity to apply their knowledge in real work situation, thereby bridging the gap between theory and practice.

  * Enlist and strengthen employers’ involvement in the entire educational process.
